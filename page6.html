What is a Research?

Research is the systematic and organized method of finding answers to questions. It is systematic because it is a process broken up into clear steps that lead to conclusions.

Research is organized because there is a planned structure or method used to reach the conclusion. Research is only successful if we find answers, whether we like these answers or not.

Development research is focused on relevant, useful, and important questions. If there are no questions, there can be no research.

Watching Television

We chose the topic of watching television because of the wide range of choices when it comes to topics within it. What interests us about it is that it tells us stories and connections that can affect us as people. Our opinion about the current issue regarding watching TV is that there are both good and bad benefits for an individual. We are also interested in learning more about this issue, as it also concerns us as students.  

The main people who we can gather information from are our classmates, we can also gather more researched causes and effects on websites and organizations that have written on this topic. As APEC Grade 10 students, this topic is highly relevant to us because we spend a significant amount of time watching television, and it is important for us to understand how this habit can affect our academic performance. The result of this study will provide us with insight on how we can make informed decisions about our television viewing habits, and potentially improve our academic performance. Additionally, understanding the consequences of excessive television viewing can help us develop healthy habits that will serve us well in the future academically. A study on the effects of television on children and adolescents has been published by the National Institute of Health (NIH).

The main research question for Exploring the Effects of Television Viewing Patterns on the Academic Progress of APEC Schools Grade 10 Students is most likely: "Does television viewing have a negative effect on academic performance among APEC Schools Grade 10 students?" This question asks the relationship between television viewing habits and academic progress. Television viewing patterns have been a topic of debate, with some arguing that it negatively impacts academic performance by consuming time and causing distractions. Others believe it can be beneficial for learning various subjects, such as science, history, and foreign languages. However, the impact of television viewing on academic performance might change depending on how it’s used, with some students using it as a learning material for their studies, while others use it as a form of escapism or distraction. 

Watching TV is known and enjoyed worldwide because it is one of the simplest and most popular types of entertainment. As of August 22, 2023, the country that watches the most television is the United States of America, with an average of 3.35 hours of daily viewing time. Based on the American Time Use Survey (ATUS), 79.2% of individuals aged 15 and above dedicate some of their day to watching TV.

The phrase "binge-watch" was first used in 2003, although the practice of watching several episodes of a show at once started to gain popularity in 2012. The first 13 episodes of the first season of House of Cards were released on Netflix in 2013 rather than one episode every week, causing a new era of binge-watching streaming material. "Binge-watch" was named the word of the year by Collins English Dictionary in 2015, citing a 200% spike in usage the year before. The typical binge lasts three hours and eight minutes, according to 73% of Americans who admit to binge-watching. 90% of millennials and 87% of Gen Z reported binge-watching, and 40% of those age groups binge-watch on average six episodes in one sitting.

Audience Analysis

We conducted an audience analysis as well as a demographic survey on the students of ONL Passion 10 2 from APEC Schools to gather their beliefs, opinions,
and attitude towards our topic.

Belief and Opinion 

The majority of students believe that the impact of TV on academic progress depends on the individual habits and abilities of the student. Our demographic believes that the effects of watching tv depend on the content watched. Most participants think that the effects of watching television on a student's academic progress are both good and bad, ultimately being neutral.

Attitude

Responses demonstrate that TV may be utilized to teach by providing examples and explanations. To give them the chance to unwind, it can also be used for entertainment.
The majority of them also stated that the way pupils manage their time will shape how viewing television affects them. But there is also a response that
claims that since no one watches TV anymore,
it might not have any effect.
